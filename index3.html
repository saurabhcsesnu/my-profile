Dr. Saurabh J. Shigwan

Assistant Professor, Department of Computer Science and Engineering

Shiv Nadar Institute of Eminence, Delhi-NCR

Welcome to my academic webpage. My current research focuses on sparse Diffusion MRI reconstruction and tractography, deep learning–based CT reconstruction from sparse sinograms, and unsupervised segmentation of medical images using graph neural networks. I teach Digital Image Processing, Deep Learning for Visual Computing, and have previously taught Discrete Mathematics and Data Structures.

Research Interests & Ongoing Projects

  • Sparse Diffusion MRI Reconstruction and Tractography
    Students: Abhishek Tiwari, Ananya Singhal (Sept ’22 – Present)
    Collaborator: Dr. Rajeev Kumar (SNU)
    Using transformer-based deep networks to reconstruct diffusion tensors and perform tractography from undersampled DWI data.
  • Unsupervised Image Segmentation using Graph Neural Networks
    Students: Kovvuri Sai Gopal Reddy, Bodduluri Saran, Mudit Adityaja (Sept ’23 – Present)
    Collaborators: Dr. Nitin Kumar, Dr. Snehasis Mukharjee (SNU)
    Developed an SOTA GNN model with modularity loss for segmentation on medical and vision datasets.
  • Deep Learning–Based CT Reconstruction
    Student: Ishaan Bharatiya (May ’24 – Present)
    Collaborators: Prof. Yogesh Rathi (HMS), Dr. Rajeev Kumar (SNU)
    Exploring DNN approaches to enhance CT images from sparse sinograms.
  • Analysis of Spine Bone for Fractures
    Student: Chekuri Arahanth Varma (Sept ’23 – Present)
    Implementing geometry-aware neural networks for sparse cone-beam reconstructions.

Interests:

Statistical Modeling, Bayesian Analysis, Medical Image Processing, Machine Learning, Computer Vision, Deep Learning, Graph Neural Networks, Shape Analysis.
